About Me

My name is Danielle Cox, and I am working on my Master of Arts degree in History, with a concentration in Public History. If all goes well, I will graduate in Spring 2022. My goal is to work in museums or in another cultural heritage position, and you can check out my related work on my portfolio or my LinkedIn.

I graduated from the College of Charleston with a Bachelor of Arts in History in 2017, and after two years working in healthcare administration I decided to go back to school to pursue work that I'm passionate about. The fact that the COVID-19 Pandemic broke out five months before classes began was just a cherry on top.

My research specialties are local Charleston history, the nineteenth century, material culture, and digital history. I don't have any formal coding or computer science trading, but I like to tinker with different technical tools. Everything on this website is 100% designed and coded by me, with the help of many, many online tutorials particularly on JavaScript.

For their support on this project, I'd like to thank my thesis advisor, Dr. Tammy Ingram, the other members of my thesis committee, Dr. Amanda Mushal and Dr. Rachel Donaldson, and Dr. Elisa Jones for her guidance on the digital components. I also owe a debt of gratitude to the unbelievable support of my aunt, April Darcy, and my close friends, particularly Sarah Davidson, Hunter Sturgill, Michael Blanton, Kelly Thompson, Nicole Sullivan, Stephanie Moore, and Mary Raycroft. No one could ask for better cheerleaders, sounding boards, and (sometimes by force) providers of a healthy work-life balance. In that vein, my therapist Delores Hollen also needs a shoutout.
